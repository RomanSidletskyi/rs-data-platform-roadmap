# Language Tool Catalog

This catalog groups language-learning tools by function instead of by brand familiarity.

The goal is to build a useful stack for real developer growth.

## 1. Translation And Rewriting Tools

### DeepL

Best for:

- precise translation
- checking wording nuance
- rewriting short professional text more naturally

Use carefully:

- good for precision
- not enough alone for speaking or retention

### ChatGPT

Best for:

- explanation of phrases or grammar in context
- rewriting text with a requested tone
- conversation practice

Use carefully:

- can over-explain
- should not replace independent writing practice

### Reverso Context

Best for:

- seeing phrases in real contexts
- distinguishing similar expressions

Use carefully:

- context examples help more than dictionary-style memorization

## 2. Reading Support Tools

### Readlang

Best for:

- reading texts with low friction
- quick word or phrase lookup during reading

### LingQ

Best for:

- extensive reading
- recurring vocabulary exposure

### Language Reactor

Best for:

- learning from subtitled videos
- combining listening and reading

## 3. Vocabulary Retention Tools

### Anki

Best for:

- long-term spaced repetition
- serious, repeatable review habits

Risk:

- easy to overload with too many low-value cards

### Mochi

Best for:

- cleaner, lower-friction spaced repetition workflows

### Quizlet

Best for:

- lighter-weight review and quick study sets

## 4. Pronunciation And Speaking Tools

### ELSA Speak

Best for:

- pronunciation feedback
- repeated speaking drills

### Speechling

Best for:

- guided speaking and correction style practice

### ChatGPT Voice

Best for:

- low-friction speaking practice
- explaining technical ideas aloud
- interview-style Q and A practice

Risk:

- helpful for fluency, but weaker than a structured pronunciation tool for detailed speaking mechanics

## 5. What To Combine

Good minimal stack:

- DeepL for precision
- ChatGPT for explanation and speaking
- Anki for retention
- Language Reactor or YouTube transcripts for listening and reading

Good writing-focused stack:

- ChatGPT for structure
- DeepL Write or Grammarly-style tools for polish
- your own developer artifacts as practice material

## Rule

The best stack is not the biggest stack.

The best stack is the smallest one you can use consistently across reading, writing, listening, and speaking.
