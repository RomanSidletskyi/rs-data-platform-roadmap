# Obsidian With AI Plugins

Obsidian-style knowledge systems with AI plugins are useful when you want a durable note layer instead of chat-only memory.

## What It Is

Use this stack to keep research notes, decision notes, reading summaries, and prompt patterns connected over time.

## Best For

- one source of truth for notes
- linking architecture ideas and research findings
- turning transient AI sessions into durable knowledge

## Not Good For

- direct code changes
- fast throwaway ideation with no need for retention

## Strengths

- strong long-term knowledge organization
- useful for decision journals and mistake logs
- helps connect notes across topics and modules

## Weaknesses

- setup and maintenance overhead
- easy to create too many notes with weak value

## Main Risks

- note sprawl
- storing AI output without synthesis

## How To Use It Well

- keep a small note schema
- capture only decisions, insights, and reusable prompts
- link notes to real projects and modules

## How Not To Use It

- do not copy long AI answers into the vault without a conclusion

## Who Benefits Most

- learners and engineers building a long-term personal knowledge system

## Primary Modes

- learning
- research
- writing

## Rule

Knowledge tools are useful only when they reduce future ambiguity.
