# Anki

Anki is strongest for long-term retention when you are disciplined about what goes into the deck.

## What It Is

Use Anki when you want repeated active recall of high-value phrases, concepts, and language patterns over time.

## Best For

- spaced repetition of technical phrases
- review of recurring English patterns
- retention of high-value concepts and vocabulary

## Not Good For

- motivation by itself
- deciding what is worth remembering
- learning through isolated low-value cards

## Strengths

- strong long-term retention mechanism
- works well for repeated technical vocabulary and phrases

## Weaknesses

- easy to overload
- quality depends entirely on card selection discipline

## Main Risks

- building a big deck of low-value cards
- memorizing single words with no context

## How To Use It Well

- store phrases and short examples, not just single words
- review a small useful deck consistently

## How Not To Use It

- do not collect vocabulary faster than you review it

## Primary Modes

- learning

## Good Card

```text
The pipeline failed because upstream schema changes were not backward compatible.
```

## Weak Card

```text
backward compatible
```

## Rule

If the deck keeps growing but review quality drops, the system is overloaded.
