# Speaking About Systems Exercise

## Goal

Practice explaining a system clearly aloud.

## Scenario

You need to explain a data pipeline or platform flow to a reviewer, interviewer, or teammate.

## Tasks

1. Write a short spoken outline.
2. Explain the system in 60-90 seconds aloud.
3. Cover the problem, components, flow, and one trade-off.
4. Ask AI to challenge your explanation and request follow-up questions.
5. Repeat with a clearer version.

## Deliverable

- spoken outline
- first explanation draft
- AI follow-up questions
- improved explanation draft

## Self-Review Rubric

Score your final version from 1 to 5:

- structure: does the explanation move logically from problem to flow to trade-off
- system clarity: can the listener follow the components and data movement
- signal-to-noise: are filler phrases removed
- trade-off quality: is at least one meaningful design compromise explained
- listener orientation: does the explanation fit a reviewer, interviewer, or teammate context

## Strong Result Looks Like

- the explanation starts with why the system exists
- the core flow is understandable without diagrams
- one important risk or trade-off is named clearly
- the wording is simple enough to say aloud naturally
