# Gemini

Gemini-style assistants are useful as broad general-purpose tools for summarization, ideation, and alternative explanations.

## What It Is

Use Gemini-style tools when you want another framing lens on a topic before committing to deeper implementation or research work.

## Best For

- summarization
- alternative explanations
- high-level comparison
- early research notes

## Not Good For

- precise repository edits
- exact code changes with local context
- final technical decisions without evidence

## Strengths

- fast reframing of topics
- helpful for comparing several approaches at a high level
- useful for creating structured notes from broad material

## Weaknesses

- can stay too high-level
- may blur important engineering details
- still requires validation for factual claims

## Best Use Cases

- summarizing broad topics quickly
- generating alternative explanations of a concept
- comparing several approaches at a high level
- helping draft structured learning or research notes

## Main Risks

- stopping at broad summarization instead of moving into evidence
- using general summaries where architecture details matter

## How To Use It Well

- use it early in the discovery phase
- ask for clear distinctions and counterexamples
- validate important claims against docs, code, or tests

## How Not To Use It

- do not hand off final design reasoning to a summary tool
- do not use it as a substitute for repository context

## Who Benefits Most

- learners exploring unfamiliar topics
- engineers who need quick framing before deeper work

## Primary Modes

- learning
- research
- writing

## Rule

Use it to widen your view first, not to skip verification.
