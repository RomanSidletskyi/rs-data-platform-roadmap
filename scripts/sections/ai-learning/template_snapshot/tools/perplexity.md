# Perplexity

Perplexity-style research tools are strongest for fast breadth-first exploration and source discovery.

## What It Is

Use this class of tool when you need to scan a topic quickly before deciding where deeper validation should happen.

## Best For

- scanning a new topic quickly
- finding sources and jumping to documentation faster
- comparing several options at a high level before deeper validation

## Not Good For

- direct code changes
- architecture decisions made without follow-up validation
- replacing documentation review in critical decisions

## Strengths

- high speed of discovery
- useful first pass for unfamiliar landscapes
- helps narrow a large topic into candidate directions

## Weaknesses

- shallow summaries can feel more solid than they are
- source quality still varies
- not enough by itself for final technical claims

## Main Risks

- treating fast research as verified fact
- copying conclusions without checking primary sources

## How To Use It Well

- use it for breadth first
- extract candidate sources and themes
- validate important claims against docs, code, or examples

## How Not To Use It

- do not end the research at the summary layer

## Who Benefits Most

- engineers exploring unfamiliar tools, practices, or architecture options

## Primary Modes

- research

## Rule

Treat fast research output as a lead, not as a final conclusion.
