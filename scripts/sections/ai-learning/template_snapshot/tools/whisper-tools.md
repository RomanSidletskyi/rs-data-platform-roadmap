# Whisper-Based Transcription Tools

Whisper-style transcription tools are useful when you want spoken material to become searchable, reviewable, and reusable.

## What It Is

Use this class of tool to turn audio, meetings, videos, or your own spoken practice into text that can be reviewed or summarized.

## Best For

- transcribing technical talks or podcasts
- turning spoken practice into review material
- extracting notes from voice explanations

## Not Good For

- architecture reasoning by itself
- source validation without reading the original material

## Strengths

- converts passive audio into reusable text
- useful for speaking feedback loops and research capture

## Weaknesses

- transcription quality varies with audio quality and terminology
- still needs human cleanup for important notes

## Main Risks

- treating rough transcripts as final notes
- missing terminology errors in technical language

## How To Use It Well

- review technical terms after transcription
- summarize the transcript into decisions, questions, and next steps
- pair it with note capture and weekly review

## How Not To Use It

- do not keep raw transcripts as the final artifact

## Primary Modes

- learning
- research
- speaking

## Rule

The transcript is raw material, not the finished note.
