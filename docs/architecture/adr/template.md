# ADR Template

## Title

Write one clear decision title.

Example: `Use Batch For Scheduled Reporting Pipelines`

## Status

Choose one:

- Proposed
- Accepted
- Rejected
- Superseded

## Context

Describe the real problem and constraints.

Questions to answer:

- what problem is being solved
- what requirements matter most
- what is failing in the current approach
- what scale, latency, trust, or governance pressure matters here

## Decision

State the decision in one or two direct sentences.

The decision should say what is being chosen, not only what is being discussed.

## Consequences

Benefits:

- benefit 1
- benefit 2
- benefit 3

Drawbacks:

- drawback 1
- drawback 2
- drawback 3

## Alternatives Considered

- alternative 1 and why it was not chosen
- alternative 2 and why it was not chosen

## Review Trigger

State what future change would cause this ADR to be revisited.

Examples:

- data volume grows beyond local processing
- latency requirement becomes near real time
- governance obligations become stricter

## Related Notes

- relevant topic README
- relevant anti-pattern note
- relevant case study
- relevant trade-off note
- relevant system-design note

## Quality Check

Before finalizing, confirm:

- the decision is explicit
- the simpler alternative was considered
- the main trade-off is visible
- the main failure or cost consequence is visible
